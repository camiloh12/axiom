I. Scope

We have examined Cloudvault Technologies Inc.'s (the "Service Organization") description of its cloud infrastructure management system throughout the period January 1, 2025 to December 31, 2025 (the "Description") and the suitability of the design and operating effectiveness of controls described therein, based on the criteria for a description of a service organization's system in DC section 200 and the applicable trust services criteria (TSC) established by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA).

The scope of our examination included security, availability, and processing integrity trust services criteria as they relate to Cloudvault Technologies' cloud infrastructure platform and associated customer data processing operations.

II. Management's Assertion

Cloudvault Technologies Inc.'s management has provided its assertion that the accompanying description fairly presents the system that was designed and implemented throughout the period January 1, 2025 to December 31, 2025. Management further asserts that the controls within the system were suitably designed and operated effectively throughout the specified period to meet the applicable trust services criteria.

III. Description of Tests of Controls

Our examination involved performing procedures to obtain evidence about the suitability of the design and operating effectiveness of the controls to meet the applicable trust services criteria. Our tests of controls were performed across 14 control objectives mapped to the SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (CC6.1, CC6.2, CC7.1, CC7.2, CC8.1 and related criteria). Test procedures included inquiry of appropriate personnel, observation of processes, inspection of documents and records, and reperformance of control activities. Population-based testing was applied where transaction volumes supported statistical sampling, with sample sizes determined by the assessed risk of the control and the frequency of the control activity.

IV. Results of Tests

Our testing of 14 controls across the trust services criteria categories identified 12 controls operating effectively throughout the examination period. Two exceptions were noted:

Exception 1 (CC6.2 — Logical Access): In 3 of 25 sampled user access reviews, the quarterly review was completed 5–8 days past the defined policy window. Management implemented an automated reminder system in July 2025, and no late reviews occurred in Q3 or Q4 2025.

Exception 2 (CC7.1 — System Operations Monitoring): One critical monitoring alert rule was inadvertently disabled for 11 days during a platform migration in March 2025. The gap was detected during an internal security review and remediated with additional change management controls for monitoring configurations.
