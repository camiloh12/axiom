Logical and Physical Access Controls

Draft v2 — Edited Auto-saved

Objective

To evaluate the design and operating effectiveness of logical and physical access controls implemented by Cloudvault Technologies Inc. to protect information assets from unauthorized access during the audit period January 1 through December 31, 2025.

Scope

This workpaper covers control CTRL-001 (Logical and Physical Access Controls), which satisfies SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC6.1 and ISO 27001 Annex A control A.8.3. Testing encompassed the full population of 847 user provisioning events during the audit period, with a sample of 25 events selected for detailed reperformance. The scope also includes inspection of the access control policy documentation and inquiry about physical access procedures.

Procedures Performed

TP-001: Inspection of Access Control Policy Documentation. Obtained and inspected the Role-Based Access Policy (v4.2, effective September 2025) [Ref: Role_Based_Access_Policy_v4.2.pdf]. The policy defines role-based access templates for all standard positions, requires documented manager approval for all provisioning requests, and establishes quarterly access review procedures. The policy was approved by the CISO on September 12, 2025.

TP-002: Reperformance of User Access Provisioning. Selected a systematic random sample of 25 from 847 user provisioning events. For each sampled user, I obtained the access request form [Ref: Access_Request_Forms_Q1-Q4_2025.pdf] and verified it against the IAM provisioning logs [Ref: IAM_System_Provisioning_Logs_2025.csv] and the current access matrix [Ref: User_Access_Matrix_2025.xlsx].

Results

TP-001 completed with no exceptions. The access control policy is current, comprehensive, and appropriately approved by senior management.

TP-002 identified two exceptions out of 25 sampled provisioning events (8% exception rate):

Exception 1 — Missing Approval Documentation: User #847-0312 (Marketing Analyst) was provisioned on March 15, 2025. While an access request form exists, it lacks the required manager approval signature. The corresponding IT service ticket references verbal approval from the department head, but per the access control policy (Section 4.2), written authorization is required prior to provisioning. I confirmed the access level granted was appropriate for the Marketing Analyst role template.

Exception 2 — Excessive Access Privileges: User #847-0589 (Sales Engineer) was provisioned on July 22, 2025 with admin-level CRM permissions that exceed the standard Sales Engineer role template. The access request form was properly approved by the sales director, but the approved access level does not match the standard role template. No documented justification for the elevated access was found in the access request, the service ticket, or the quarterly access review documentation.

Conclusion

Based on the procedures performed, the logical and physical access control design is effective. The access control policy is comprehensive and current. However, operating effectiveness is not fully effective due to two provisioning exceptions identified in the sample of 25 (8% exception rate). The exceptions indicate control gaps in two areas: (1) enforcement of the documented approval requirement, and (2) validation of requested access against standard role templates. These findings should be communicated to management for remediation consideration and may require disclosure in the SOC 2 report if determined to be material.
