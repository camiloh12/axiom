Objective

To evaluate the design and operating effectiveness of logical and physical access controls implemented by Cloudvault Technologies Inc. to protect information assets from unauthorized access during the audit period January 1 through December 31, 2025.

AI Generated

Scope

This workpaper covers control CTRL-001 (Logical and Physical Access Controls), which satisfies SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria CC6.1 and ISO 27001 Annex A control A.8.3. Testing encompassed the full population of 847 user provisioning events during the audit period, with a sample of 25 events selected for detailed reperformance.

AI Generated

Procedures Performed

TP-001: Inspection of Access Control Policy Documentation. Obtained and inspected the current Role-Based Access Policy (v4.2), which defines role templates, the provisioning workflow requiring manager approval, and access level restrictions. The policy was last updated September 2025 and approved by the Chief Information Security Officer.

TP-002: Reperformance of User Access Provisioning. Selected a systematic random sample of 25 from 847 user provisioning events during the audit period. For each sampled event, verified: (a) existence of a documented access request form, (b) manager approval signature, (c) access granted matches the approved request, and (d) access level is appropriate for the user's assigned role template.

AI Generated

Results

TP-001 was completed with no exceptions. The access control policy is comprehensive, current, and appropriately approved.

TP-002 identified two exceptions out of 25 sampled provisioning events:

Exception 1: User #847-0312 (Marketing Analyst, provisioned March 15, 2025) had an access request form present but lacked the required manager approval signature. The IT ticketing system referenced verbal approval only.

Exception 2: User #847-0589 (Sales Engineer, provisioned July 22, 2025) was granted admin-level CRM permissions exceeding the standard Sales Engineer role template. No documented justification for the elevated access was identified.

AI Generated

Conclusion

Based on the procedures performed, the logical and physical access controls are designed appropriately. However, two exceptions were identified in operating effectiveness related to the user provisioning process. These exceptions indicate that while the access control policy is well-defined, adherence to the approval workflow is not consistently enforced. Management should evaluate whether additional controls or monitoring are needed to ensure provisioning approvals are properly documented.
